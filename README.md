# Phaser-Cinematic

I'll explain what I did to fulfill each of the 8 requirements.

## 3 scenes:
1. Studio intro cinematic.
2. Loading screen.
3. Title screen.

## 2 audio assets:
I included 2 whoosh sound effects which played in harmony together. And 1 printer sound effect, which was played during the loading screen. I did not make any of the audio assets, I found them through Wikimedia Commons.

## 2 image assets:
I used an image of a hand pointing and comic style text background, both for the title screen. The hand pointing was for a selection indicator for the menus, and the text background was for some splash text. I did not make the images, and found them both through image searches that were filtered for transparent images under public domain.

## 2 geometric shapes:
For the loading screen I put a lot of overlapping lines which where animated to spin around for some slight visual interest as the game loads. I put similar diagonal lines on the title screen, as well as a retangular gradient background.

## LOGOTYPE TEXT!!!
I made my own logotype text using Inkscape. I am familiar with Inkscape, so it wasn't too difficult.

In the slides prototype the logo simply comes in from the side, and then fades out. I could potentially animate something more interesting, like the text starts from just one letter, and the rest pops out or something, but only if time allows.

## 1 multi-line text block:
There are a few multi-line text elements on the title screen, like the selecting menu, or the splash text.

## 1 fading scene transition:
After the studio logo appears there is a fade out to white.

I didn't consider while making it, that maybe the point was to have the scene after that one fade in from white, like a seemless cut. But I don't feel like that's really commonly done anymore, a fade out and in like that is kind of just like a cut that takes longer.

## 1 object motion animation:
The studio logo slides in from the side of the screen, there is also object motion on the loading screen.
